**Held by:** [ASPIRE](https://www.aspireuae.ae/), United Arab Emirates<br>
**Duration:** March 2023 – February 2024<br>
**Location:** Abu Dhabi, United Arab Emirates

## Overview

The [3rd Mohamed Bin Zayed International Robotics Challenge (MBZIRC)](https://www.mbzirc.com) is one of the world's premier robotics competitions. Our team from KAIST developed a fully autonomous multi-robot system comprising:

- Multiple **Unmanned Aerial Vehicles (UAVs)**
- One **Unmanned Surface Vehicle (USV)**

The mission simulated a real-world maritime security scenario — **detecting illegal vessels** and **retrieving stolen objects** from those vessels in a GPS-denied marine environment.

## Technical Challenges

- Operating without GPS in open water environments
- Coordinating air and surface vehicles in real time
- Detecting and tracking targets on moving vessels
- Precise manipulation and retrieval in challenging sea conditions

## Technical Approach

- **Localization:** AprilTag-based visual localization and Optical Flow for GPS-denied navigation
- **Mission Planner:** High-level task management and multi-robot coordination
- **Control:** Robust flight and surface vehicle control under maritime disturbances
- **Object Detection & Tracking:** Real-time detection of targets and objects of interest

## Achievement

🥈 **2nd Place** – 3rd Mohamed Bin Zayed International Robotics Challenge (MBZIRC 2024)
